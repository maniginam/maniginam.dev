How to plan a wedding

Quick answer

To plan a wedding, set your budget and guest count first, then book the venue — it locks your date and drives every other vendor. Book photographer, caterer, and music next, send invitations about eight weeks out, and confirm the final headcount in the last month.

The month-by-month timeline

WhenWhat to do
12+ months outSet the budget and guest count, lock the date and region, decide the vibe, draft the guest list, and book the venue — it drives every other date.
9–11 months outBook the highest-demand vendors: photographer, caterer, band or DJ, and florist. Book the officiant and send save-the-dates.
6–8 months outBook videographer, cake, hair & makeup, and transportation. Reserve a room block and order invitations.
4–5 months outFinalize the ceremony order, book rentals, schedule the hair & makeup trial, and order favors.
2–3 months outMail invitations (about 8 weeks out), do the catering tasting, buy the bands, and start alterations.
1 month outConfirm the final headcount, build the seating chart, confirm timing with every vendor, and pick up the marriage license.
Week ofGive final meal counts to the caterer, send the day-of timeline to everyone, and pack an emergency kit.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I start planning a wedding?

Most couples plan over 9 to 12 months, and the venue should be booked first because it locks in your date and drives every other vendor. Shorter engagements work too — you just compress the early phases.

What should I book first?

Book the venue first. It sets your date, capacity, and budget anchor, and the highest-demand vendors (photographer, caterer, band) book up around the same date, so secure those next.

Can I plan a wedding without a planner?

Yes. A clear timeline, a budget breakdown, and a vendor checklist cover most of it. Bridezilla does that work for you and drafts the vendor emails, so you get a planner's output without the planner's fee.
