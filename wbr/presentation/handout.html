What AI Actually Is

Autocomplete on steroids. It predicts the next word based on billions of pages of text. Not a brain. Not alive. A very fast pattern matcher.

Golden Rule: Use AI like a smart intern — helpful and fast, but always check their work.

What AI Is Good & Bad At

Good at:

  • Drafting emails, letters, and social media posts
  • Summarizing long documents
  • Translating jargon to plain English
  • Brainstorming and planning
  • Answering questions patiently

Bad at:

  • Facts — makes things up ("hallucinations")
  • Math — surprisingly unreliable
  • Recent events — knowledge has cutoff dates
  • Legal/medical advice — not a professional
  • Your specific situation — doesn't know you

Stay Safe

NEVER give AI: Social Security numbers, passwords, bank info, medical records, private photos, or client data (on free tiers).

Rule of thumb: If you wouldn't post it on Facebook, don't put it in free AI.

Scam alert: "AI detected a problem with your account" = always a scam. AI doesn't monitor your accounts.
VOICE CLONING: AI can clone anyone's voice from 20 seconds of audio. FBI reports $893M in losses. If a "family member" calls asking for money — hang up and call them back. Set up a family code word tonight.

For Parents

  • Ages 8–12: Use AI together as a family. Never share name, school, or photos.
  • Ages 13–17: "Explain this" = good. "Write my essay" = not. Check school policy.
  • Key question to ask your kid: "Show me how you use ChatGPT"
Tip: Stay curious, not scared. Parents who engage protect better than parents who ban.
